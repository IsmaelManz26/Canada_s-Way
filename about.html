Our Institutions

Parliament Hill

Parliament of Canada

The Parliament of Canada is the legislative body of the country and is composed of two chambers: the House of Commons and the Senate. The House of Commons is the main source of Canadian legislation and plays a fundamental role in political decision-making and government oversight.

On the other hand, the Senate is the upper chamber and is made up of senators appointed by the Governor General on the advice of the Prime Minister. The Parliament meets in the Parliament Buildings in Ottawa, an iconic structure that symbolizes Canada's history and values.

Supreme Court of Canada

Highest Judicial Authority

The Supreme Court of Canada is the highest judicial authority in the country, responsible for interpreting and upholding the Constitution. The Court also plays a crucial role in protecting the rights and freedoms of Canadians through its decisions.

Located in Ottawa, the Supreme Court building is a symbol of justice and democracy. It is composed of nine justices, including the Chief Justice, who are appointed by the Governor General on the advice of the Prime Minister.

Bank of Canada

Canada's Financial Anchor

The Bank of Canada is the country's central bank, responsible for maintaining economic stability and promoting sustainable growth. It manages monetary policy, issues currency, and oversees the financial system to ensure the stability of the Canadian economy.

Located in Ottawa, the Bank of Canada plays a vital role in controlling inflation, supporting employment, and maintaining the overall stability of the national economy. Through its strategic actions and policies, it helps create a strong, resilient, and secure financial foundation that benefits all Canadians.

Key Figures of Canada

Justin Trudeau

- Prime Minister of Canada

Leader of the Liberal Party

Justin Trudeau has been the Prime Minister of Canada since 2015, leading the Liberal Party. He was first elected as a Member of Parliament in 2008, and in 2015, he achieved a historic victory. His government has promoted progressive policies focused on equality, climate change, and social inclusion.

Commitment to Diversity

Trudeau has been a strong advocate for diversity and human rights, implementing policies that promote gender equality and the rights of Indigenous communities. His approach has helped transform Canada into a more inclusive and respectful country.

Response to the Pandemic

During the COVID-19 crisis, Trudeau led the country by implementing economic support programs and a successful vaccination campaign. While he received praise for his response, he also faced criticism for the management of certain aspects of the pandemic.

Mary Simon

- Governor General of Canada

First Indigenous Governor General

Mary Simon is the Governor General of Canada, appointed in 2021. She made history as the first Indigenous person to hold the position. As Governor General, she serves as the representative of the British monarch in Canada and performs ceremonial duties while upholding Canada’s constitutional framework.

Advocate for Indigenous Rights

Throughout her career, Simon has been a strong advocate for Indigenous rights and the preservation of Indigenous cultures. She has worked extensively to raise awareness about the challenges facing Indigenous communities in Canada, and her appointment as Governor General highlights Canada’s commitment to reconciliation.

Leadership in Diplomacy and Social Issues

Before becoming Governor General, Mary Simon had a distinguished career in diplomacy and public service. She served as Canada’s Ambassador for Circumpolar Affairs and worked on social issues related to Indigenous peoples. Her leadership continues to inspire Canadians toward unity and progress in the areas of culture and governance.
