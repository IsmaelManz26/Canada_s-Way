  • Early Canada

    Indigenous Peoples & First Contacts

    Before European arrival, Canada was home to diverse Indigenous cultures with rich traditions, languages, and trade networks.

    In the late 15th century, John Cabot landed on Canada's east coast, marking the start of European exploration.

    The early 1600s saw the establishment of New France with the founding of Quebec by Samuel de Champlain.

  • European Exploration

    First Encounters and New Frontiers

    In 1497, John Cabot became one of the first Europeans to reach Canada, opening the door to further exploration.

    In 1534, Jacques Cartier sailed the St. Lawrence River, claiming the land for France, laying the foundation for French settlement.

    The establishment of the New France colony in the early 1600s, marked a significant era in Canada's history with strong ties to Europe.

  • Confederation

    The Formation of Canada

    In 1867, the British North America Act united the provinces of Ontario, Quebec, New Brunswick, and Nova Scotia to form the Dominion of Canada.

    The creation of Confederation marked the beginning of Canada's journey as a self-governing country within the British Empire.

    Over time, more provinces and territories joined, expanding Canada into the nation we know today.

Guardians of the Arctic: Canada's Polar Bears

Found primarily in the Arctic regions of Manitoba, Nunavut, and the Northwest Territories, polar bears thrive in the vast, icy wilderness. As apex predators, they symbolize the strength and fragility of the Arctic ecosystem.

Visitors to Canada can witness these incredible animals in their natural habitat, offering a rare glimpse into the beauty of the wild and the importance of conservation efforts to protect them for future generations.

Canada Today

A Nation of Growth and Opportunity

Canada is a global leader in innovation and economic resilience, driven by industries like technology, green energy, and natural resources. Its cities are vibrant centers of commerce, while rural areas contribute with sustainable development and agriculture.

Renowned for its diversity, Canada is home to people from over 200 ethnic origins. With English and French as official languages, the country thrives on multiculturalism and inclusivity, fostering a united and respectful society.

Canada prioritizes environmental sustainability, leading efforts to combat climate change and protect its vast natural landscapes. From the Rockies to the Arctic, it balances development with conservation to secure its future.

Canada's cuisine reflects its diverse culture, blending influences from Indigenous, French, British, and immigrant traditions.
